## The 10-20-30 Concept

Each 1-minute cycle consists of three phases performed **back-to-back** with no rest:

| Phase | Duration | Intensity | Effort Level |
|-------|----------|-----------|--------------|
| **Low** | 30 sec | <30% of max | Easy / recovery |
| **Moderate** | 20 sec | ~60% of max | Steady / comfortable |
| **High** | 10 sec | 90-100% of max | All-out sprint |

- 5 cycles = **1 block** (5 minutes continuous)
- 2-4 blocks per session, separated by **2-3 min rest**
- Total session time: **20-35 min** including warm-up

## Rowing Machine Adaptation

### Intensity Translation to Rowing

| Phase | Duration | Stroke Rate (spm) | Pace (approx.) | Damper Feel | Description |
|-------|----------|-------------------|-----------------|-------------|-------------|
| **LOW** | 30 sec | 16-20 spm | Easy paddle | Light arms only | Relaxed recovery strokes, minimal leg drive |
| **MODERATE** | 20 sec | 22-26 spm | Normal training pace | Full stroke, steady | Your comfortable long-distance rowing pace |
| **HIGH** | 10 sec | 30-38+ spm | Max sprint | Explosive full body | Maximum power and rate, legs-back-arms chain |

### Key Rowing Cues Per Phase

**30 seconds LOW:**
- Arms-only or light legs-and-arms strokes
- Focus on breathing, posture, and recovery
- Let heart rate drop slightly

**20 seconds MODERATE:**
- Full rowing stroke at your "easy 5K pace"
- Controlled, rhythmic, sustainable effort
- Maintain good technique

**10 seconds HIGH:**
- Explosive leg drive, powerful back swing, fast arms
- Maximum stroke rate and power output
- Sprint as hard as possible

## Training Programs

### Program A: Beginner (Weeks 1-4)

For sedentary or untrained individuals. Based on patient-group protocols (Baasch-Skytte 2020, Ehlers 2020).

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Frequency** | 2-3 sessions per week |
| **Warm-up** | 5-8 min easy rowing at 18-20 spm |
| **Blocks** | 2 blocks (Weeks 1-2), 3 blocks (Weeks 3-4) |
| **Rest between blocks** | 3 minutes light paddle or stop |
| **Cool-down** | 3-5 min easy rowing + stretching |
| **Total session time** | ~20-28 min |
| **Weekly high-intensity time** | 3.3-5.0 min |

### Program B: Intermediate (Weeks 1-7)

For recreational rowers or fitness enthusiasts. Based on the original Gunnarsson & Bangsbo (2012) protocol.

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Frequency** | 3 sessions per week |
| **Warm-up** | 8-10 min progressive rowing |
| **Blocks** | 3 blocks (Weeks 1-4), 4 blocks (Weeks 5-7) |
| **Rest between blocks** | 2 minutes light paddle |
| **Cool-down** | 5 min easy rowing + stretching |
| **Total session time** | ~28-35 min |
| **Weekly high-intensity time** | 7.5-10.0 min |

### Program C: Advanced / Athletic (Weeks 1-8)

For trained rowers or athletes. Based on Hostrup et al. (2019) and Skovgaard et al. (2024).

| Component | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| **Frequency** | 3 sessions per week (+ 1 optional steady-state row) |
| **Warm-up** | 10 min with 3-4 race-pace bursts |
| **Blocks** | 3 blocks (Weeks 1-4), 4 blocks (Weeks 5-8) |
| **Rest between blocks** | 2 minutes active paddle |
| **Cool-down** | 5-8 min easy rowing + mobility work |
| **Total session time** | ~30-40 min |
| **Weekly high-intensity time** | 7.5-10.0 min |

## Practical Guidelines

### Before You Start

- Get medical clearance if you have any cardiovascular conditions, diabetes, or asthma
- Perform a baseline test: 2K erg test or 5K erg test for comparison
- Record resting heart rate and blood pressure if possible
- Familiarize yourself with the erg and good rowing technique

### Damper Setting

| Phase | Suggested Damper (Concept2) | Rationale |
|-------|---------------------------|-----------|
| LOW (30s) | 3-4 | Light resistance for easy recovery |
| MODERATE (20s) | 3-5 | Normal training resistance |
| HIGH (10s) | 3-5 | Keep same setting; power comes from rate + force |

> Keep the damper setting consistent throughout. The intensity difference comes from
> stroke rate and force application, not damper changes. A setting of 3-5 on a
> Concept2 is suitable for most people.

### Warm-Up Protocol

| Time | Activity | Stroke Rate |
|------|----------|-------------|
| 0-3 min | Easy rowing, arms only | 16-18 spm |
| 3-5 min | Half-slide rowing | 18-20 spm |
| 5-7 min | Full-slide easy rowing | 20-22 spm |
| 7-8 min | 2-3 short pickups (5 sec each) | 26-30 spm |

### Common Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Why It Matters | Fix |
|---------|---------------|-----|
| Going too hard in the 30s LOW phase | Prevents recovery, defeats the protocol | Truly paddle easy, near-idle |
| Not going hard enough in the 10s HIGH phase | Misses the key stimulus | Sprint at genuine 90-100% effort |
| Skipping warm-up | Injury risk, suboptimal performance | Always do 8+ min warm-up |
| Changing damper between phases | Wastes time, disrupts rhythm | Keep damper constant |
| Training more than 4 days/week | Overtraining risk, diminishing returns | Stick to 2-3 sessions + optional steady |
| Poor technique at high rate | Injury risk to lower back | Maintain sequential legs-back-arms chain |

### Safety Notes

- Stop immediately if you feel chest pain, dizziness, or unusual shortness of breath
- Asthma patients: take 2 puffs of beta-2 agonist 10-15 min before training
- Hypertensive patients: monitor blood pressure before and after sessions initially
- Diabetic patients: check blood glucose before and after; have fast-acting carbs available
- Compliance in studies was >80% with **no adverse events** reported across all patient groups
